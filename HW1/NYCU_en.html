NYCU

National Chiao Tung University traces its origins back to "Nanyang Public School," founded in 1896 with the approval of Sheng Xuanhuai (1844-1916), a Qing official advocating for reform, who established the school in Shanghai. This marked the beginning of the Shanghai school, focusing on subjects related to shipping, postal services, and commerce. In 1920, under the leadership of the Minister of Transport, Ye Gongchuo (1881-1968), multiple schools under the Ministry were merged to form Chiao Tung University, solidifying its expertise in engineering, science, and management. In 1937, the university, along with others across the nation, came under the Ministry of Education and was renamed "National Chiao Tung University.

After a century of development in Taiwan, Chiao Tung University transitioned from a purely engineering institution to a comprehensive university. Before its merger with Yang Ming University in 2021, it housed a total of 12 colleges, including Electrical Engineering, Computer Science, Science, Engineering, Management, Humanities, Life Sciences, Hakka Studies, Optoelectronics, Technology Law, International Semiconductor Industry, and Smart Science and Green Energy, with a student population reaching 13,000.

Yang Ming Chiao Tung University was established in 2021 through the merger of Yang Ming University and Chiao Tung University. Previously, both universities excelled in their respective fields of biomedicine and electrical engineering; the merger has created a complementary relationship. The newly formed Yang Ming Chiao Tung University comprises 9 campuses and 20 colleges, with nearly 20,000 students, making it one of Taiwan's largest research universities. With mutual agreement, the university aims to integrate biomedicine, electrical engineering, and artificial intelligence, focusing on advancing digital biomedical research and education, striving to lead in the revolution of smart biomedical technology. (resource: NYCU)

Department of Computer Science

National Chiao Tung University has long been recognized as an outstanding research university, and its information field is a premier cradle for cultivating top-notch computer technology talent in the country. The department boasts a remarkable history: it established Taiwan's first computer research program in 1960, introduced the nation's first computer in 1961, and successfully developed the first electronic computer in Taiwan in 1971. It also created the world's first stamp recognition system, the first 3D computer vision car, and the oldest Chinese keyboard globally.

The Department of Computer Science and Engineering at Yang Ming Chiao Tung University is the largest information department in Taiwanese universities, featuring the highest number of faculty members, the most comprehensive research areas, the largest network of alumni, and unparalleled computer lab facilities. The department integrates the university's abundant and exceptional talent and resources in the information field, aiming to reach international excellence and cultivate the next generation of elite information professionals.

For university students, access to quality learning resources and environments is crucial. In addition to having one of the country’s leading academic networks and libraries, our department offers a computer center, software and hardware integration labs, and various unique spaces for student use. These facilities not only allow students to quickly find the information they need but also provide opportunities for hands-on practice and collaboration with peers. In recent years, the department has been renovating classroom facilities to enhance the learning environment, optimizing spaces to foster effective teaching and learning.

The student association organizes a variety of vibrant activities, including dynamic sports competitions, annual student-led camps, and the Computer Science Camp. Additionally, there are exciting large-scale events such as company visits, welcome tea parties, hot pot gatherings, and the Drama Cup.
